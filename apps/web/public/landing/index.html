Why we built it

A real four, tired of the alternatives.

We are a padel four in the North East, plus a few who always want in. Nobody argues, we just could never cleanly settle who sits out.

The apps already out there never quite hit it. Playtomic got a lot of us into padel and books a court well, but it can be confusing, and finding and filling a game is harder than it should be.

When your crew is bigger than four, fairly filling that last spot is a game of Mastermind. The Rotation handles it automatically now, instead of living on somebody's spreadsheet.

Ratings are open and transparent. Turning up, or not, is part of the record.

Circles let you draft in the players who raise the quality of a game, and make them easy to find again.

Still the same four. Still sorting who sits out, just without the ninety messages.

What sent us looking

The trouble with the other apps.

Playtomic got a lot of us into padel, and it is genuinely good at booking a court. The friction starts with everything around the game.

The problem

A rating nobody can explain

Your number moves and you are not told why. Down 0.01 with no reason, win-or-lose maths that dings you for a partner's off night, and a coaching recalibration that can quietly set you back.

In CUATRO

Glass, and the Ledger

Every point of movement is written down in plain English and never edited. Beat a stronger pair 6-3 6-4 and you see exactly what that was worth, and who you played.

The problem

Level anxiety, as standard

Inflated numbers the moment you sign up, then a social feature that tells you what percentage of players reckon you are lower than your rating. Everyone is on edge before they have hit a ball.

In CUATRO

No questionnaire, no rounding up

You start Unrated and play three games first. Your number only ever comes from real, confirmed results. It is allowed to go up, and it is nobody's business but yours.

The problem

A marketplace of strangers

When you just want your usual four, you get open matches with people you have never met, and no easy way back to your own group.

In CUATRO

Circles and the Fourth Call

Your group is the home screen. Short a player, CUATRO asks the closest people first: your circle, then people you have played with, then players nearby at your level. Closest people first, strangers never, unless you choose it.

The problem

The group chat where plans go to die

The game invite is buried under off-topic noise, and nobody can actually tell who is coming until an hour before.

In CUATRO

The Standing Game

Your weekly fixture runs itself. It opens the RSVP, holds the four, queues the reserves, and promotes the next person in when someone drops. No poll, no chasing.

The problem

Fees, ads, and the upsell

Booking commission, service fees, a premium tier for things you expected to be free, and at one point a floated charge just for logging a match you played somewhere else.

In CUATRO

None of that

CUATRO is free. No ads, no upsell, no fees. We never touch your court money and never hold a penny of it. The Tab just keeps score.

The features

Everything the four needs, nothing it doesn't.

The Rotation · now live

So we ended the dance.

The hard part was never an argument, it was working out who sits out. Turn the Rotation on for a game and nobody races for a slot. Everyone marks that they are available, and CUATRO picks the fairest four.

Available
Everyone taps that they are free for the game, instead of first come, first served.
Fairest four
Fewest recent games go on first, and whoever sat out last week is due. Early weeks run first come while the history builds.
The cutoff
The organiser sets how long the window runs. At the cutoff, four or fewer available means everyone plays, more and the Rotation decides.
Every pick
Comes with its reason shown, so it is plainly fair.

Nobody chases, nobody does the polite dance, everybody gets their turn.
